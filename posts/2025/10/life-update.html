Life Update

Vim Lena
October 11th, 2025

Hey, it’s been a minute since I updated this site so I thought I would do a life update type post and just talk about I’ve been up to

Job/School Update

Mid-August, I was unexpectedly laid off of my job because of AI automating my role and the company downsizing the department I worked in because of this. It’s a scary time to be unemployed as a queer trans person with the state of everything as of late, feels like a lot of companies out there view trans people as sort of a liability or “issue.” I don’t think I’ve ever known so many people who are unemployed at one time since I have entered the labor force as there are now. This also occurred just as I was about to start attending college again. I very briefly went to college about a decade ago but dropped out before getting any sort of degree. Part of my decision to return to school was feeling secure enough at my (now previous) job that I could juggle it and school at the same time. This is a prospect I wasn’t looking forward to at all, but I was willing to navigate if my job was at least predictable. As it turns out, not everything happens as you predict.

For a period of time I got very depressed after being laid off. I debated not going to school this year because of not having a very solid foundation to start the year on, but at this point I had already picked my classes so I elected to just go anyway. Maybe I could use this time where I don’t have to deal with work to give me some time to remember how to do things like studying or managing my time while doing assignments and projects. I looked for a job on the side but mostly focused on school for the time being, and eventually I was able to snag some various interviews. It feels like being unemployed is a full time job, ironically enough. Anyway, eventually I got a position at a local non-profit doing actually really great work and offering me very reasonable benefits, pay, and hours. The unemployment horror was coming to an end and I could finally allow myself to breathe again.

School has been somewhat challenging to get back into and I have changed the program I’m part of since starting in the early fall. Initially I wanted to be a nurse but I eventually realized that the nursing program was not compatible with me working full-time during regular hours so I had to pivot to something more compatible with my schedule. Eventually I decided on a Computer Science and IT type program mostly focused on Networking technologies that would lead me to eventually have a position like Network Admin or something along those lines. My thought with selecting this program is that a lot of technology stuff is going to be replaced by AI, but that AI cannot do things like physically setting up a network or firewall. Also as everything increasingly becomes more and more focused on the internet and more people are connected to each other than ever before, making sure networks are configured correctly, secure, and running well would be important. I have been a computer nerd for as long as I have been around computers and it just seems like a logical progression to move towards doing computer stuff as a job.

Anyway, all of that said, I haven’t been updating my website because I’ve been unable to stop moving forward or I would drown (like a shark) lately. Between starting a new job and starting school, while navigating having no insurance, being in a band, having friendships and relationships, etc. I have literally never been so busy as I am now.

LenaMono

I had talked about the monospace typeface Iosevka in my last typography post. I got around to looking at some of the various customization options for generating variants of Iosevka, and quickly had a realization that it was just as much a monospaced typeface development framework as it was a typeface. The About section on its GitHub repo being “Versatile typeface for code, from code” clicked and I decided to try my hand at generating my own customized version.

Several different revisions later and a fair amount of trial and error, I am announcing my own distribution of Iosevka called LenaMono. The biggest inspiration for the glyph choices I made come from Meslo LG, as it and DejaVu Sans Mono have long been my personal favorite monospaced typefaces for programming. I was also informed by Inconsolata LGC as well as Source Code Pro, both are personal favorites of mine.

I have gathered that programming ligatures are a bit of a controversial subject among programmers; I think they’re visually nice, but also respect those who prefer to have their code represented literally. With this in mind, I created L (ligatures) and NL (no ligatures) versions of the typeface so that I wasn’t forcing my preference there on anybody. I also opted to include three different widths to allow for saving horizontal space if preferred or required: a regular width, a SC (semi-condensed) width, and a C (condensed) width. This all means that LenaMono actually has 6 variations represented by the letters appended to the LenaMono name. For example, if you wanted to use LenaMono with no ligatures and a semi-condensed width, you would list the name in your editor as LenaMonoNLSC (No Ligatures, Semi-Condensed). If you wanted a regular version with ligatures, you would use LenaMonoL (Ligatures). If you wanted to use the condensed version with ligatures, you would use LenaMonoLC. I opted for generating the font files in discrete widths and with ligatures on or off so that people could easily select the version they preferred or worked for what they were doing. I know that most software nowadays lets you pick a variant from a dropdown menu or an attribute in a configuration file, but, to me, this just seemed like an easier approach.

All the private-build-plans.toml files for the variants are included in their respective folders which you can use to generate your own variants of Iosevka if you copy it to the downloaded/copied Iosevka repo and run a command in it. For the details of that, check out the Iosevka Customizer page.

I have also patched each variant with Nerd Font symbols, they’re included in the NerdFonts folder inside of each variant. To use the Nerd Font version of LenaMono, select the name of the variant you want and type Nerd Font after it (like LenaMonoNLSC Nerd Font).

I’m going to make a separate post when I release LenaMono publicly so keep an eye out for that.

Helix Editor and Zed

After being a long-time Vim (and NeoVim) user, I have migrated over to using the Helix Editor. I was initially unsure about it as many of the bindings for navigating and editing text were similar to Vim/NeoVim but slightly different in a way that was a bit difficult to wrap my head around. As I actually used it for a longer period of time, however, I realized that they were actually really good and made sense as a design choice. Helix is free and open source, written in Rust, and runs in the terminal. Rather than relying on plugins for many things expected of a modern editor, Helix takes a “batteries included” approach and allows for you to configure your settings in a simple toml file for anything that doesn’t just work out of the box. There are a lot of really well-designed quality of life improvements compared to NeoVim and Helix is quickly becoming one of my essential programs as I use it for more and more stuff.

I still sometimes use a GUI editor for just quick edits or looking at files when I’m not in the terminal, for this I have started to use Zed instead of my old go-to Sublime Text. I appreciate how fast and easy to configure Zed is, it even has a fairly comprehensive Helix mode built-in. Zed seems to push AI coding, which I am not personally a fan or and don’t use, but thankfully you can turn this all off in the settings with a simple "disable_ai": true in the config file. Zed, like Helix, is free, open source, and made with Rust.

I have coincidentally been looking into learning Rust with the intention of using it as I move into more network-oriented computer projects because of its security and excellent speed. Rust seems really powerful, but getting the hang of it seems like a bit of a daunting task. I am slowly working my way up to that by just reading more about it and exposing myself to the syntax and approaches to problem solving it utilizes. Pretty crazy how Mozilla dissolved the Rust development branch just the language started to take off also, kinda fumbled the ball there.
